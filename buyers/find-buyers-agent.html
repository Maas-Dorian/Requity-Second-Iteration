Find a buyer agent who understands your needs

To find a buyer agent who understands your needs, look for three things: real experience in the neighborhoods and price range where you are buying, a clear process for comps and offers, and a communication style that matches how you ask questions and make decisions.

Buying a home involves dozens of decisions under time pressure, and your buyer agent is the person guiding nearly all of them. This guide covers what a buyer agent should actually do for you, how they help with comps and offers, why communication style matters more for buyers than most people expect, and how Requity helps buyers find an agent who fits.

What a buyer agent should do

  • Learn your goals, budget, and non negotiables before showing you anything
  • Surface homes early and evaluate each one honestly, including reasons not to buy
  • Run realistic comps so you offer with evidence, not emotion
  • Design offer strategy for your market: price, terms, timing, and contingencies
  • Coordinate inspections, appraisal, financing milestones, and closing
  • Communicate at the pace of your market and your comfort level

How buyer agents help with comps

A good buyer agent runs comparable sales before you offer, adjusting for condition, lot, and street level differences, and tells you what a home is worth rather than what it is listed for. That number anchors everything: your opening offer, your ceiling, and your walk away point. Ask any candidate agent to walk you through a recent comp analysis; the clarity of the answer is a preview of your future offers.

Comps also protect you from the two classic buyer mistakes: overpaying in a bidding war because emotions took over, and losing the right home over a few thousand dollars because nobody showed you what it was actually worth.

How buyer agents help with offers

Offer strategy is where a buyer agent earns their fee. In a competitive market: how to structure price and terms so your offer wins without overpaying, when escalation makes sense, and which contingencies to keep because the risk is real. In a slower market: how hard to negotiate, what to ask for in credits and repairs. A good agent explains the reasoning behind every recommendation, so you decide with your eyes open.

Why communication matters for buyers

Buyers live at the speed of the market. Homes appear and go under contract in days, and offer deadlines do not wait for unreturned calls. You need an agent whose responsiveness matches your market and whose explanations match your experience level. A first time buyer paired with a terse, assume you know everything agent will feel lost; an experienced buyer paired with an over explainer will feel slowed down. Fit works both ways; see finding a real estate agent who communicates well.

Frequently asked questions

How do I find a buyer agent?

Use referrals, local brokerages, directories, open houses, and agent matching platforms like Requity that consider your communication style and buying needs. Then interview candidates about comps, offer strategy, and communication.

How do I choose a buyer agent?

Consider local market knowledge, availability, how clearly the agent explains each step, how well their communication style fits yours, and how they support you through offers and negotiation.

Can first time home buyers use Requity?

Yes. The Requity assessment captures how much guidance and explanation you want, which is especially useful for first time home buyers who want an agent who explains things clearly.

Does Requity guarantee a perfect buyer agent match?

No. Requity does not guarantee a perfect match. Requity uses assessments, communication insights, and human review to support a more thoughtful agent fit.
