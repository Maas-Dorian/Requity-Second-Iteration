Find a buyer agent who understands your needs

Buying a home involves dozens of decisions, from neighborhoods and budgets to inspections, offers, and closing. A good buyer agent guides you through each step. The right buyer agent also communicates in a way that fits you: how much detail you want, how quickly you like to decide, and how much reassurance you need when things get stressful.

What to look for in a buyer agent

  • Local market knowledge in the area where you want to buy
  • Clear explanations of each step, especially for first time home buyers
  • Responsiveness that matches the pace of your market
  • A communication style that fits how you ask questions and make decisions
  • Comfort guiding you through offers, inspections, and negotiation

If you are still comparing your options, start with where to find good real estate agents.

How Requity helps home buyers

Requity asks buyers to complete a short relationship style assessment that captures how you communicate, how you handle decisions, and what kind of support you want during your purchase. Requity then supports agent matching based on personality insights, your buying needs, and location. A Requity reviewer looks at every profile before a match is finalized. Frequently asked questions

How do I find a buyer agent?

You can find a buyer agent through referrals, local brokerages, directories, open houses, and agent matching platforms like Requity that consider your communication style and buying needs.

How do I choose a buyer agent?

Consider local market knowledge, availability, how clearly the agent explains each step, how well their communication style fits yours, and how they support you through offers and negotiation.

Can first time home buyers use Requity?

Yes. The Requity assessment captures how much guidance and explanation you want, which is especially useful for first time home buyers who want an agent who explains things clearly.

Does Requity guarantee a perfect buyer agent match?

No. Requity does not guarantee a perfect match. Requity uses assessments, communication insights, and human review to support a more thoughtful agent fit.
