How first time home buyers can choose a real estate agent

First time home buyers should choose an agent who teaches as they go: someone patient with questions, clear about each step, honest about tradeoffs, and communicative enough that you never wonder what happens next. The right agent turns an intimidating process into a manageable one.

This guide explains why guidance matters more on a first purchase, the questions that reveal whether an agent genuinely works well with first time buyers, how to set communication expectations, the red flags specific to inexperienced buyers, and how Requity matches on exactly these qualities.

Why first time buyers need guidance

Everything in a first purchase is new: pre approval, earnest money, contingencies, inspection responses, appraisal gaps, closing costs. An experienced buyer navigates these on autopilot; a first time buyer needs each one explained at the moment it matters. Agents vary enormously in their willingness and ability to teach. Choosing one who enjoys working with first time buyers, and can prove it, is the single highest leverage decision you will make in the process.

Questions to ask

  • How many first time buyers have you worked with in the past year?
  • How do you explain the process to someone doing this for the first time?
  • What do you do when a client needs more time or explanation than average?
  • How do you help buyers stay inside their budget when inventory is tempting?
  • Can you walk me through what happens between an accepted offer and closing?

The complete interview checklist is in questions to ask a real estate agent.

Communication expectations

Set them explicitly. Tell candidate agents you will have questions, ask how they prefer to receive them, and confirm how quickly they respond. A good first time buyer agent will welcome this conversation; it makes their job easier too. Be honest about your style: if you process decisions slowly, or need reassurance under pressure, say so. The right agent adapts, and the wrong one reveals themselves.

One practical habit worth requesting: a short summary of next steps after every milestone, in writing. For a first purchase, knowing what happens next is half the battle against anxiety, and an agent who provides it without being reminded is worth keeping.

Red flags

  • Impatience with basic questions during the interview stage
  • Jargon with no explanation, or answers that assume knowledge you do not have
  • Pressure to stretch your budget or waive protections without discussing risk
  • Vague answers about the steps between offer and closing
  • Slow responses while they are still trying to win your business

Frequently asked questions

How should a first time home buyer choose a real estate agent?

Prioritize teaching ability and communication fit alongside local experience. Interview several agents, ask how they work with first time buyers specifically, and choose the one whose explanations you actually understood.

What should a first time buyer expect from an agent?

Clear explanations of every step, patience with questions, honest budget guidance, realistic comps, protective offer strategy, and proactive updates from offer to closing.

Is a top producing agent the best choice for a first time buyer?

Not automatically. High volume agents can be excellent, but some delegate heavily or move too fast for a first purchase. Fit and attention matter more than production trophies for a first time buyer.
