Find an agent to sell your house

To find an agent to sell your house, build a shortlist from referrals and local research, interview at least three listing agents, and compare their pricing strategy, marketing plans, and communication styles. The agent you choose will guide decisions that directly affect your sale price and timeline.

This guide covers what separates a strong seller agent from an average one, how to run the comparison, why strategy and communication matter so much for sellers specifically, and how Requity helps sellers find an agent whose working style fits.

What to look for in a seller agent

The essentials: recent listings sold in your area and price band, a pricing analysis they can defend comp by comp, a concrete marketing plan, proactive communication, and a negotiation approach you trust. Beyond the essentials, look for honesty in the pitch: the agent willing to tell you your home needs paint, or that your price expectation is high, is showing you how they will represent you to buyers.

How to compare listing agents

Interview them the same week so comparisons stay fresh, ask each the same core questions, and compare answers on specificity rather than confidence. The agent comparison framework and the listing agent guide give you the full question set. Do not skip references or recent seller outcomes: list to sale price ratio and days on market tell you a lot.

Why strategy matters

Selling well is a sequence of strategic calls: price position, preparation spend, launch timing, offer deadlines, and how to respond when the market pushes back. Agents with real strategy think in sequences and contingencies. Agents without it list the home and hope. Ask every candidate what they do in week three if showings are slow; the answer separates the two groups instantly.

Strategy also matters at the offer table. A strong agent evaluates offers on total strength: financing type, appraisal risk, contingencies, and timeline flexibility, not just the headline number. The highest offer that falls apart in escrow costs more than a slightly lower one that closes.

Why communication matters

Your home sale runs on information you cannot see directly: showing feedback, buyer agent chatter, market response. Your only window is your agent's reporting. Choose someone who volunteers information at the frequency and depth you want, and who delivers hard news straight. A seller kept in the dark makes worse decisions at every stage.

Hard news matters most. If the market is telling your agent the price is wrong, you need to hear it in week two, not week eight. Ask each candidate how they deliver feedback a seller does not want to hear, and choose the one whose answer you believe.

Frequently asked questions

How do I find an agent to sell my house?

Build a shortlist from referrals, local brokerage research, and matching platforms like Requity, then interview at least three listing agents about pricing, marketing, and communication before signing anything.

What does a good seller agent do differently?

They defend their price with comps, run a sequenced marketing plan, report proactively, evaluate offers on strength rather than just price, and tell you the truth even when it is unwelcome.

How much does a listing agent cost?

Commissions vary and are negotiable, and structures have evolved in recent years. Ask each candidate to explain their fee and what it covers, and compare value rather than fee alone.

Can Requity match me with a listing agent?

Yes. Sellers complete the relationship style assessment, and Requity supports a match based on compatibility, selling goals, and location, with human review before it is finalized.
