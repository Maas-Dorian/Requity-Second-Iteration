Where to find good Real Estate Agents

You can find good real estate agents through personal referrals, local brokerage websites, real estate agent directories, real estate marketplaces, open houses, neighborhood signs, and agent matching platforms. Each source has strengths and blind spots, and the best approach usually combines two or three of them.

Here is what each option gives you, what it misses, and how relationship based matching through Requity fits into the picture.

Referrals

Referrals from people who recently bought or sold are the most trusted source for a reason: someone you know watched this agent work under real conditions. The limitation is sample size. A referral reflects one transaction and one personality pairing. Always interview a referred agent the same way you would interview a stranger, and confirm they know your target area and price range.

Local brokerage websites

Brokerage sites list active agents by office and often include bios, specialties, and recent listings. They are useful for finding agents who focus on your specific area. Keep in mind the brokerage presents every agent positively, so treat these pages as a starting roster rather than a ranking.

Real Estate Agent directories and real estate marketplaces

Directories and large marketplaces let you filter by location and see reviews and sales activity. They are good for volume: you can build a candidate list quickly. The tradeoff is that profiles are optimized to look good, reviews skew positive, and nothing in a directory tells you how an agent communicates under pressure or whether their working style fits yours.

Open houses and neighborhood signs

Open houses let you watch an agent work in person before any commitment: how they engage visitors, how they answer questions, and how well they know the property and the street. Yard signs tell you who is actually active in a neighborhood. Both are slower methods, but they give you unfiltered, real world impressions that no profile page can.

Agent matching platforms

Matching platforms ask about your transaction and connect you with agents, usually based on sales volume and location. They save time and can surface strong performers. Most of them, however, match primarily on production numbers. High sales volume does not guarantee the agent will communicate the way you need or give you the level of attention you want.

Frequently asked questions

Where can I find good real estate agents?

The main sources are referrals, local brokerage websites, real estate agent directories, real estate marketplaces, open houses, neighborhood signs, and agent matching platforms like Requity. Combining a personal source with a research source usually works best.

What is the best place to find a real estate agent?

There is no single best place. Referrals give trust, directories give volume, open houses give firsthand impressions, and matching platforms give speed. Requity adds communication style and compatibility, which the other sources do not measure.

Are agent directories reliable?

Directories are reliable for confirming an agent is licensed and active, and for building a candidate list. They are less reliable for judging fit, since profiles are self presented and reviews skew positive. Interview before you decide.

How is Requity different from a directory?

Requity is not a list of agents to browse. It uses relationship style assessments from both clients and agents to support matching based on communication fit, transaction needs, and location, with human review.
