Find a Real Estate Agent you can feel confident moving forward with

To find a real estate agent who fits you, look beyond experience and sales numbers. The right agent communicates in a way that helps you feel informed, supported, and confident: the right amount of detail, the right pace, and the right kind of guidance for how you make decisions.

This page explains what agent fit actually means, what buyers and sellers should each look for, and how Requity helps match clients with agents based on communication style, transaction needs, and location.

What makes a good agent fit

A good fit is the overlap of three things: competence, coverage, and compatibility. Competence means real transaction experience and local knowledge. Coverage means the agent actively works your market and price range. Compatibility is the piece most searches skip: whether the agent's communication style, pace, and working approach match what you need during one of the largest financial decisions of your life.

Most people can verify the first two with research. Compatibility is harder to see from a profile page, which is why so many people end up with an agent who looks great on paper and feels wrong in practice. The rest of this page focuses on that third dimension, because it is the one you will live with every day of your transaction.

Why communication style matters

Buying or selling a home involves weeks or months of updates, questions, decisions, and negotiation moments. Some clients want detailed explanations before every step. Others want short, direct recommendations. Some need calm reassurance when a deal gets stressful; others want data and speed. When an agent's natural style matches yours, every one of those interactions gets easier. When it does not, even a highly productive agent can leave you feeling unheard.

If communication is your top priority, see the dedicated guide to finding a real estate agent who communicates well.

What buyers should look for

Buyers should look for an agent who explains the process clearly, responds at the pace of the local market, helps run realistic comps, and guides offer strategy without pressure. First time buyers usually benefit from an agent who teaches as they go. The buyer agent guide and the guide to choosing a buyer agent cover this in detail.

What sellers should look for

Sellers should look for a clear pricing methodology, a concrete preparation and marketing plan, and straightforward communication about offers and negotiation. The way an agent explains their strategy in the first meeting is usually a preview of how they will communicate all the way to closing. See finding a listing agent for the full checklist.

How Requity works

Requity is a real estate brokerage and referral based matching platform that starts with you rather than with a list of agents. You complete a short relationship style assessment covering how you communicate, how you make decisions, and what support you need. Agents on Requity complete their own assessment and receive an agent archetype describing their working style. A Requity team member reviews every profile, and matching considers personality insights, transaction needs, and market: buyers are matched in the market where they want to buy and sellers in the market where they want to sell. Frequently asked questions

Where can I find good real estate agents?

You can find good real estate agents through referrals, local brokerages, real estate agent directories, real estate marketplaces, open houses, and agent matching platforms like Requity.

What should I look for in a real estate agent?

Look for local experience, communication style, responsiveness, trust, transaction knowledge, and whether the agent's working style fits how you make decisions.

Is Requity an agent directory?

No. Requity is different from a simple agent directory because it combines a client assessment, a reviewed matching process, and a brokerage and referral workflow. The goal is to help buyers and sellers connect with agents whose market focus, communication style, and support approach fit the client's needs.

Can Requity help buyers and sellers?

Yes. Requity is designed for buyers, sellers, and people who are buying and selling at the same time.

Does fit really matter if an agent has great sales numbers?

Yes. Sales volume shows production, but it does not tell you how the agent communicates, how much attention you will get, or whether their style matches yours. Fit determines what the experience feels like day to day.
