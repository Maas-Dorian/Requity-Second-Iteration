Finding an agent

How do I find a good real estate agent?

Combine sources: referrals from recent buyers or sellers, local brokerage research, and directories to build a shortlist, then interview at least three agents. Compare their local knowledge, communication, and strategy before deciding. The full process is in how to find a good Realtor.

Where do most people find their Realtor?

Most people use a referral from someone they know, and many others find agents through directories, marketplaces, open houses, or matching platforms. Referrals are a strong start but reflect one transaction and one personality pairing, so an interview is still worthwhile.

How many real estate agents should I interview?

Three is a practical minimum. The differences between agents in communication, local knowledge, and strategy usually become obvious by the second or third conversation, and you cannot see those differences with a single candidate.

Should I hire a friend or family member as my agent?

Only if they would win the job on merit against other candidates. Mixing a major financial transaction with a personal relationship makes honest feedback and hard conversations harder in both directions. At minimum, interview others for comparison.

How do I check if an agent is licensed?

Every state has a license lookup through its real estate commission or licensing board, and many directories display license status. Confirm the license is active and check for disciplinary history.

What does it cost to use a real estate agent?

Agent compensation is commission based, negotiable, and has evolved in recent years, so ask each candidate to explain their fee and what it covers. Starting the Requity client assessment is free for buyers and sellers.

Matching with an agent

Is there a way to get matched with a Realtor?

Yes. Agent matching services pair you with agents based on your answers about the transaction, and some also consider fit. Requity matches on communication style, transaction needs, and location, with human review. See the full answer.

How do agent matching platforms decide who to match me with?

Most rank agents on production data: recent sales volume, location coverage, and response speed. Requity adds relationship style assessments from both the client and the agent, so communication fit is part of the match. More in real estate agent matching.

Do matching services sell my contact information?

Some lead referral services do send your information to multiple paying agents. Before using any service, ask how it is compensated and how many agents will receive your details. Requity does not sell your information to lists of agents.

Am I obligated to work with a matched agent?

No. A match is an introduction. Interview the matched agent the way you would any candidate, and move forward only if the fit feels right.

Buyer agents

What does a buyer agent actually do?

A buyer agent finds and evaluates homes against your goals, runs comparable sales so you offer with evidence, designs offer strategy, and coordinates inspections, appraisal, and closing. The full picture is in find a buyer agent.

How do I choose a buyer agent?

Compare candidates on local knowledge, availability, offer strategy, communication style, patience, and fit with your goals. Interview at least three. The checklist is in how to choose a buyer agent.

Do first time home buyers need a different kind of agent?

Not a different license, but a different style: someone patient, clear, and willing to teach each step as it happens. See the first time home buyer agent guide.

Can I work with more than one buyer agent at once?

Practically and ethically, no, especially if you have signed a buyer representation agreement. Choose carefully up front instead; it is better for you and fairer to the agents.

My agent only sends me listings I already saw online. Is that normal?

Portals surface most inventory now, so overlap is normal. What your agent should add is judgment: local insight, honest evaluation of each home, early information, and offer strategy. If nothing is added beyond links, raise it directly.

Listing agents

How do I find an agent to sell my house?

Build a shortlist from referrals and local research, then interview at least three listing agents about pricing methodology, marketing plans, and communication. The process is detailed in find an agent to sell your house.

Should I pick the agent who suggests the highest listing price?

Usually not. Some agents win listings with flattering prices, then walk them down after weeks of stagnation, which typically nets less than pricing correctly at launch. Choose the best defended price, not the highest one.

What should a listing agent's marketing plan include?

Preparation and staging advice, professional photography, listing copy, syndication, open house strategy, and a plan for what changes if showings are slow. Specifics are in how to choose a listing agent.

How long is a typical listing agreement?

Three to six months is common. Ask about cancellation provisions before signing, and be cautious of unusually long terms with no exit.

What if I need to sell and buy at the same time?

Tell every candidate agent up front, because sequencing two transactions changes strategy. The Requity assessment adapts when you are doing both, and matching considers coverage of both markets.

Comparing agents

How do I compare real estate agents?

Score each candidate on five dimensions: local experience, recent sales history, communication style, process and strategy, and personal fit. The framework is in how to compare real estate agents.

What matters more: sales volume or fit?

You need baseline competence, and beyond that, fit usually determines the quality of your experience. A high volume agent who communicates in a way that frustrates you is a bad trade for a slightly smaller producer who fits.

Are online agent reviews trustworthy?

Directionally useful, but they skew positive and rarely describe communication style or fit. Use reviews to screen out consistent problems, not to make the final choice; interviews tell you more.

What questions reveal the most in an agent interview?

Ask them to walk you through a recent comp analysis and a difficult negotiation. Specific, story backed answers signal real experience. The full list is in questions to ask a real estate agent.

Should I compare agents from different brokerages?

Compare individuals, not brands. Agents within one brokerage vary as much as agents across brokerages, so build your shortlist around people whose recent work matches your area and price range.

Communication and fit

Why does communication style matter in choosing an agent?

A transaction is months of updates, questions, and decisions under pressure. When the agent's style matches yours, every interaction builds confidence; when it does not, friction compounds into distrust. It is the most common reason agent relationships break down.

How can I tell if an agent communicates well before hiring them?

Watch response speed during the interview stage, ask about update frequency and channels, and notice whether they listen and summarize next steps. Details in find a Realtor who communicates well.

What is agent client compatibility?

It is how well an agent's communication style, pace, decision guidance, and support approach fit a specific client. Two clients can have opposite experiences with the same agent. More in agent client compatibility.

What is a real estate personality assessment?

A short set of questions about how you communicate, decide, and handle stress during a transaction, used to identify what kind of agent relationship fits you. See real estate personality assessment.

Can I find an agent who fits my communication style?

Yes. You can test for it in interviews with direct questions about update frequency and responsiveness, or use Requity, which measures communication style on both sides and makes it a core matching input.

Red flags and switching

What are the biggest red flags in a real estate agent?

Poor communication, pressure without explanation, comps they cannot defend, vague local knowledge, no clear process, and not listening to your goals. The full list is in real estate agent red flags.

My Realtor does not communicate. What should I do?

Raise it once, directly and specifically: what you need, how often, and through which channel. If nothing changes, review your agreement terms and consider switching. Communication rarely improves on its own mid transaction.

Should I fire my Realtor?

Try one honest conversation first; some problems are fixable. If they persist, check your representation or listing agreement for term and cancellation provisions, put your decision in writing, and involve the brokerage if needed.

How do I switch real estate agents?

Review any signed agreement, communicate your decision in writing, and ask the brokerage about a release if time remains on the term. Then take what you learned about your needs into the next search so the same mismatch does not repeat.

Is it a red flag if an agent pressures me to sign quickly?

Pressure without explanation is. Real urgency exists in fast markets, but a good agent explains the reason. An agent rushing you into a representation agreement before answering your questions is prioritizing their timeline over your outcome.
